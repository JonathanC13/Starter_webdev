Chester Bennington

His music touched the hearts of millions

Linkin Park's lead vocalist Chester Bennington.

Timeline of Chester Benningtion:

  • 1976 - Born in Phoenix, Arizona
  • 1983 to 1993 - Endures horrific abuse and turns to alcohol and hard drugs.
  • 1993 - Moves in with his mother where she helps him with his drug addiction. He begins working at Burger King and starting his career as a professional musician in a band called Sean Dowdell and His Friends?. Later in the year, Dowdell and Bennington left and formed a new band named Grey Daze.
  • 1998 - Leaves Grey Daze, struggles to find a new band.
  • 1999 - Joins the band Xero, the name changed to Hybrid Theory, and then finally to Linkin Park which signed with Warner Brothers.
  • 2000 - Releases the album Hybrid Theory, 4.8 million copies sold in its debut week.
  • 2002 - Releases the album Reanimation, 270,000 copies sold in its debut week.
  • 2003 - Releases the album Meteora, 810,000 copies sold in its debut week.
  • 2004 - Side projects with other artists like DJ Lethal and Jay-Z. Created a side band named Dead by Sunrise. Raised money for victims of Hurricane Charley, Indian Ocean tsunami, and the Special Operations Warrior Foundation. Founds the non-profit Music for Relief Fund to respond to natural disaters.
  • 2005 - Raised money for the victims of Hurricane Katrina.
  • 2007 - Releases the album Minutes to Midnight. 625,000 copies sold in its debut week.
  • 2009 - Releases the album A Thousand Suns. 241,000 copies sold in its debut week.
  • 2012 - Releases the album Living Things. 223,000 copies sold in its debut week.
  • 2012 - Releases the album Recharded. 223,000 copies sold in its debut week.
  • 2014 - Releases the album The Hunting Party. 110,000 copies sold in its debut week.
  • 2017 - Chester Bennington sadly takes his own life at the age of 41.

"I have been able to tap into all the negative things that can happen to me throughout my life by numbing myself to the pain so to speak and kind of being able to vent it through my music."

Chester Bennington
